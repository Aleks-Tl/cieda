huluku

CIEDA’s Huluku is an annual digital art and design competition created to promote true and positive reflections of our diverse societies — a concept known as authentic representation.

It was born out of recognition that stock libraries which provide free or chargeable images, illustrations and other content just aren’t currently able to provide something which reflects everyone. We aren’t all the same in the real world so it’s now time that we celebrate our diversity within the digital art and design world.

The Huluku competition is open for everyone, everywhere to create a piece which they feel represents diverse and inclusive society, natural environments or simply people being themselves. There are lots of different subjects which will really challenge expert digital artists and designers as well as budding talent looking to get their work noticed.

Each year a selection of the best work is collated into an art book as well as made available for free for people to use within their own work, businesses and organisations. The first, second and third placed entrants will also see their work available on stock library sources which are free for as many people as possible to access and employ.
